About Rage Against the Machine
Rage Against The Machine was formed in Los Angeles, California, in the early '90s. The band consists of vocalist Zack de la Rocha, guitarist Tom Morello, drummer Brad Wilk, and bassist Tim Commerford.
Rage Against the Machine exploded onto the scene with their self-titled debut album in 1992. Hits like "Killing in the Name," "Bulls on Parade," and "Guerrilla Radio" encapsulated the band's ethos, combining blistering guitar work with Zack de la Rocha's passionate delivery, addressing social and political issues head-on. Rage Against the Machine's music was a call to action, a soundtrack for rebellion against injustice and oppression.
Since then, Rage Against the Machine has become one of the most influential rock bands of all time. They've mastered their unique sound, consisting of heavy metal, rap, punk rock, and funk influences, and further made a name for themselves with their revolutionary political views and activism.
In October 2000, Zack de la Rocha announced his departure from the band, leading to Rage Against the Machine's breakup from 2000-2007. The band eventually reunited to headline the final day of Coachella 2007 and continued to tour around the world before taking another brief hiatus.
In 2020, Rage Against the Machine announced their reunion for their “Public Service Announcement Tour” - their first show together in nine years. The dates and venues of their world tour were initially scheduled for 2020. However, they were eventually postponed to 2022 due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
Not only has Rage Against the Machine achieved significant critical and commercial success, the band has also captivated audiences with their live performances. Known for their relentless energy and fiery stage presence, the brand brings unapologetic energy to every stage they play, including stadium tours, festivals, and more.
